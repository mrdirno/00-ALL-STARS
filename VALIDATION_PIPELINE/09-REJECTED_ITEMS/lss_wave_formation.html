About: Large-Scale Structure Wave Formation

This simulation models the formation of large-scale cosmic structures based on the theory of 3D standing waves in the early universe. Particles (representing matter) are initially distributed randomly and then influenced by a potential field derived from the superposition of cosmic-scale standing waves.


The mathematical model displaces particles towards regions of constructive interference (antinodes of energy, or specific nodal surfaces of the wave function), forming patterns reminiscent of cosmic filaments and voids.


Key Concepts:

  • Standing Waves: Superposition of waves creating stable patterns of nodes and antinodes.
  • Wave Numbers (Modes): Control the complexity and scale of the wave patterns along each axis. Higher numbers mean more, smaller structures.
  • Amplitude: Determines the strength of the wave's influence on particle positions.

This visualization is inspired by research suggesting wave mechanics play a crucial role in the universe's structure, such as the work by E. Chaliasos (2005) on standing waves and P. Coles on wave-mechanical approaches to structure formation.
